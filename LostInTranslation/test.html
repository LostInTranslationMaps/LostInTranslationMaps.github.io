Minimalist Metro Poster — Concept Gallery

Ten directions to turn a functional metro map into a gallery-grade print.

Pure Line Geometry

45° / 90° discipline

Straighten the network into perfect angles, keep dots tiny, let whitespace breathe.

Less is more: draw only what you must.

Typographic Focus

Words as shapes

Treat station names as the composition. The “map” emerges from hierarchy & alignment.

Choose 2 sizes: one hero word, smaller supporting stops.

One-Line Minimalism

Single hero line

Focus on one line; label only termini. Everything else whispers.

Gorgeous as a set: one poster per line.

Monochrome with Single Accent

Gallery-grade

Everything in grey/black — one accent color for the hero line or nodes.

Swap the accent per city/series.

Circular / Spiral Layout

Geometric re-compose

Ignore geography; celebrate structure. The ring becomes the composition.

Works great for cities with a belt/ring line.

Floating Lines

Modular islands

Each line as its own “island,” arranged for balance, not geography.

Great for posters where balance > accuracy.

Architectural Blueprint

Technical calm

Ultra-thin lines, faint construction grid, tiny measurements as decorative detail.

Swap background to charcoal for a print-friendly variant.

Abstract Reduction

No labels

Only lines & dots. The title carries recognition. Zero typography in the map.

Perfect for “art print” positioning.

Color-Blocked Background

Bold field

Let a single color field do the heavy lifting. High contrast lines on top.

Swap field to NS yellow / Stockholm blue etc.

Typographic Grid Poster

Conceptual matrix

Stations as chips; small bars indicate lines. Conceptual, clean, modular.
